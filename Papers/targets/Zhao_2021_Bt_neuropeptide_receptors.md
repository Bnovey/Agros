# Genome-Wide Identification of Neuropeptides and Their Receptor Genes in *Bemisia tabaci* and Their Transcript Accumulation Change in Response to Temperature Stresses

**Authors:** Zhao T, Zhao S, Feng Y, et al.
**Journal:** *Insect Molecular Biology* 30(2): 123–135, 2021
**Link:** https://doi.org/10.1111/imb.12677

## Abstract

Comprehensive genome-wide annotation of neuropeptides and their receptors in the *B. tabaci* MEAM1 genome identified 35 neuropeptide receptor genes including biogenic amine receptors (octopamine, tyramine, dopamine, serotonin), FMRFamide receptors, and sNPF receptors. Expression profiling under heat/cold stress showed octopamine and tyramine receptors are significantly upregulated under stress conditions. No invertebrate-specific GPCR families had vertebrate counterparts.

## Takeaways

- Confirms octopamine/tyramine GPCR family is present and active in *B. tabaci* — extends the OAR selectivity argument
- Genome-wide annotation provides the full target landscape for neuropeptide GPCRs in whitefly
- AlphaFold models available for all 35 receptor genes via MEAM1 proteome
- Temperature-stress upregulation suggests octopamine receptors may be particularly important under field conditions
- Monoterpene insecticides (thymol, carvacrol) exploit insect OARs with known field-relevance
