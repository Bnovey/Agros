# Dual Mutations in the Whitefly Nicotinic Acetylcholine Receptor β1 Subunit Confer Target-Site Resistance to Multiple Neonicotinoid Insecticides

**Authors:** Mao K, Zhang Y, Crissman J, et al.
**Journal:** *PLOS Genetics* 20(2): e1011163, 2024
**Link:** https://doi.org/10.1371/journal.pgen.1011163

## Abstract

Field populations of *Bemisia tabaci* with dual mutations A58T+R79E in the nAChR β1 subunit show broad cross-resistance to imidacloprid, thiamethoxam, clothianidin, and acetamiprid. The mutations introduce electrostatic repulsion at the orthosteric agonist-binding site, reducing neonicotinoid binding affinity >100-fold without impairing octopamine-mediated activation. Functional expression in *Xenopus* oocytes confirmed that WT BTβ1 is essential for neonicotinoid sensitivity.

## Takeaways

- First confirmed target-site resistance to neonicotinoids in *B. tabaci* — landmark paper
- A58T+R79E precisely localize the agonist-binding interface: residues 58 and 79 are in the extracellular N-terminal domain
- These resistance mutations define the binding pocket geometry for peptide binder design
- Selectivity: insect nAChR β subunits are structurally distinct from vertebrate muscle/neuronal nAChR subunits
