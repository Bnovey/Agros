# Influence of Three Insecticides Targeting GABA Receptor on Fall Armyworm *Spodoptera frugiperda*: Analyses from Individual, Biochemical and Molecular Levels

**Authors:** Lv C, Zhang J, Bao H, et al.
**Journal:** *Pesticide Biochemistry and Physiology* 179: 104971, 2021
**Link:** https://doi.org/10.1016/j.pestbp.2021.104971

## Abstract

Electrophysiology in *Xenopus* oocytes confirmed SfRdl1 is highly sensitive to fluralaner and fipronil (IC50 ~5 nM), and moderately sensitive to endosulfan. Field populations show A302S resistance mutation reduces fluralaner/fipronil IC50 by 30–50-fold. Whole-organism bioassays confirmed that GABA receptor-targeting insecticides remain efficacious against susceptible *S. frugiperda* but not A302S mutants.

## Takeaways

- SfRdl1 IC50 ~5 nM for fluralaner/fipronil — among the most pharmacologically potent insect receptor interactions known
- A302S resistance mutation maps the pore binding site (same position as PxRdl1 A302S) — conserved across Lepidoptera
- High potency at low concentration = a validated target for tight-binding peptide design
- GABA-A cryo-EM templates (PDB: 6HUG) applicable; AlphaFold model for SfRdl1 available
