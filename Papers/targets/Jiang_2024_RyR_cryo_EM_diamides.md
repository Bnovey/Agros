# Cryo-EM Structures of Ryanodine Receptors and Diamide Insecticides Reveal the Mechanisms of Selectivity and Resistance

**Authors:** Jiang D, Tonggu L, Bhatt A, et al.
**Journal:** *Nature Communications* 15: 9339, 2024
**Link:** https://doi.org/10.1038/s41467-024-53490-0

## Abstract

Cryo-EM structures of insect RyR bound to chlorantraniliprole and flubendiamide were resolved, revealing the diamide-binding site in the transmembrane gating region. The structures explain the mechanism of insect selectivity (insect-specific residues lining the binding site) and resistance (I4790K/G4946E disrupt diamide contacts). Comparison with mammalian RyR confirms the diamide site is structurally absent in vertebrate channels.

## Takeaways

- First cryo-EM structures of insect RyR with diamides bound — defines the transmembrane binding pocket geometry at near-atomic resolution
- Complements Zhao 2018 (PxRyR NTD, PDB: 5Y9V) — together these provide full structural coverage of PxRyR from NTD to transmembrane pore
- Insect-specific residues in the diamide site are directly confirmed — strong safety rationale
- Resistance mutations (I4790K, G4946E) disrupt specific contacts — design peptides that avoid those contact points to maintain efficacy across resistant populations
- Use this structure directly for BoltzGen transmembrane domain targeting
