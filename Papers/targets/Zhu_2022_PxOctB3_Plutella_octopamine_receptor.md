# Pharmacological Properties and Function of the PxOctβ3 Octopamine Receptor in *Plutella xylostella*

**Authors:** Hang Zhu, Zheming Liu, Haihao Ma, Wei Zheng, Jia Liu, Yong Zhou, Yilong Man, Xiaoao Zhou, Aiping Zeng  
**Journal:** *Insects* 13(8):735, 2022  
**Link:** https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC9409995/

## Abstract

The diamondback moth (*Plutella xylostella*) is a destructive lepidopteran pest of cruciferous vegetables that has developed resistance to over 90 insecticide types. This study cloned and characterized the β-adrenergic-like octopamine receptor PxOctβ3, confirming it as a member of the Octβ3 protein family. Expression analysis revealed the receptor is present throughout all developmental stages.

When expressed in HEK-293 cells, octopamine successfully activated PxOctβ3, increasing intracellular cAMP levels. Naphazoline was the most effective agonist, while metoclopramide and mianserin had significant antagonistic effects. Yohimbine and phentolamine showed no significant inhibition.

RNAi experiments demonstrated that suppressing PxOctβ3 expression accelerated pupation in larvae. The findings indicate PxOctβ3 regulates developmental processes in *P. xylostella* and represents a viable target for pest management strategies.
