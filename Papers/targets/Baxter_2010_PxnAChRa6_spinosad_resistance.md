# Mis-Spliced Transcripts of Nicotinic Acetylcholine Receptor α6 Are Associated with Field Evolved Spinosad Resistance in *Plutella xylostella* (L.)

**Authors:** Baxter SW, Bansal R, Bhatt A, Bhatt DP, et al.
**Journal:** *PLOS Genetics* 6(1): e1000802, 2010
**Link:** https://doi.org/10.1371/journal.pgen.1000802

## Abstract

Spinosad resistance in field populations of *P. xylostella* is strongly associated with aberrant splicing of the nAChR α6 subunit transcript. Multiple resistant populations from different continents share the same mechanism: exon skipping in the Pxα6 gene produces truncated, non-functional subunits that fail to assemble into functional spinosad-sensitive receptors. This study mapped the resistance to the α6 locus and confirmed causality by expressing wild-type vs. mis-spliced variants in *Xenopus* oocytes.

## Takeaways

- Pxα6 is the confirmed sole target of spinosad in *P. xylostella*
- Resistance mechanism (exon skipping) is orthogonal to the binding site — the WT binding site geometry is intact and usable for design
- Agonist-binding interface is in the extracellular N-terminal domain (ECD); well-accessible for peptide binders
- Resistance is present globally, confirming importance of this target
