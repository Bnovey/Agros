# Effect of Insulin Receptor on Juvenile Hormone Signal and Fecundity in *Spodoptera litura* (F.)

**Authors:** Chen Z, Fan X, Zhang Y, et al.
**Journal:** *Insects* 13(8): 701, 2022
**Link:** https://doi.org/10.3390/insects13080701

## Abstract

RNAi knockdown of the insulin receptor (InR) in *Spodoptera litura* (closely related to *S. frugiperda*) suppressed JH III synthesis via the IIS→FOXO axis, reduced vitellogenin (Vg) protein levels, and caused underdeveloped ovarioles with a severe reduction in egg output (>70% reduction in fecundity). InR knockdown did not affect larval survival — effect is primarily on reproduction.

## Takeaways

- Confirms InR as a fecundity/reproduction target in Spodoptera — disruption dramatically reduces egg output without killing adults
- Reproductive disruption is a viable biopesticide mode of action: reduces next-generation population without requiring acute toxicity
- Effect mediated through IIS→FOXO→JH III axis — upstream disruption has broad downstream consequences
- Extracellular L1-CR-L2 ectodomain is the peptide design target; insect InR L2 and fibronectin III domains have divergent sequences vs. human InR
- Human InR ectodomain template: PDB 2DTG, 3LOH; focus peptide design on insect-divergent residues in L2 domain for selectivity
- Note: study is in *S. litura* not *S. frugiperda* directly — extrapolation is strong but not exact
