# A Short Neuropeptide F Analog (sNPF), III-2 May Particularly Regulate Juvenile Hormone III to Influence *Spodoptera frugiperda* Metamorphosis and Development

**Authors:** Shu B, Zhao S, Li Y, et al.
**Journal:** *Pesticide Biochemistry and Physiology* 195: 105542, 2023
**Link:** https://doi.org/10.1016/j.pestbp.2023.105542

## Abstract

An sNPF peptide analog (III-2) was synthesized and bioassayed against *S. frugiperda* larvae. LC50 = 18.7 mg/L; sublethal doses delayed metamorphosis and suppressed JH III titers. SfsNPFR expression analysis confirmed the receptor is expressed throughout larval development with peak levels in the brain. Receptor knockdown by RNAi phenocopied the III-2 peptide treatment.

## Takeaways

- Proof-of-concept that a peptide agonist/antagonist at SfsNPFR has in vivo insecticidal activity — validates peptide binder approach directly
- sNPF signaling links to JH III axis — disruption has downstream developmental consequences beyond just receptor binding
- LC50 = 18.7 mg/L is moderate; peptide binders designed by BoltzGen for tight binding at the orthosteric site should exceed this significantly
- sNPFR is a Class A GPCR; design peptide mimics that bind the extracellular binding site and act as antagonists
- No mammalian ortholog for sNPFR — full insect selectivity
