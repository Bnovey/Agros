# Distinct Roles of Two RDL GABA Receptors in Fipronil Action in the Diamondback Moth (*Plutella xylostella*)

**Authors:** Li Q, Schiff NM, Huang K, et al.
**Journal:** *Insect Science* 29(1): 101–113, 2022
**Link:** https://doi.org/10.1111/1744-7917.12892

## Abstract

*P. xylostella* is unique among insects in expressing two Rdl paralogs (PxRdl1, PxRdl2) with divergent pharmacology. When expressed in *Xenopus* oocytes, PxRdl1 is ~40× more sensitive to fipronil than PxRdl2 (IC50: 12 vs. 480 nM). GABA sensitivity is similar between the two. RNAi silencing of each paralog separately vs. together demonstrates they have non-redundant physiological roles. The A302S resistance mutation reduces PxRdl1 fipronil sensitivity without abolishing GABA gating.

## Takeaways

- Px-specific two-paralog system: a uniquely exploitable feature for biopesticide selectivity
- PxRdl1 is the high-sensitivity fipronil target; PxRdl2 may be a resistance escape valve
- Druggable surfaces: extracellular GABA-binding domain and transmembrane pore entrance (picrotoxin/fipronil site)
- Resistance mutation A302S in PxRdl1 precisely localizes the pore-lining binding site (position 2' of TM2) — useful for design guidance
