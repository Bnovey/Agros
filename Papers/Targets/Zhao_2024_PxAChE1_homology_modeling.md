# Resistance Mechanism of *Plutella xylostella* (L.) Associated with Amino Acid Substitutions in Acetylcholinesterase-1: Insights from Homology Modeling, Docking and Molecular Dynamic Simulation

**Authors:** Zhao P, Cao Y, Liu J, et al.
**Journal:** *Insects* 15(3): 144, 2024
**Link:** https://doi.org/10.3390/insects15030144

## Abstract

Homology models of PxAChE1 were built using *Torpedo californica* AChE (PDB: 1ACJ) and validated by MD simulation. Resistance mutations A298S and G324A alter the geometry of the active-site gorge, reducing OP and carbamate binding affinity. Docking studies show that A298S widens the gorge entrance, reducing steric occlusion, while G324A shifts the acyl-binding pocket conformation. Both mutations are compatible with catalytic activity but reduce inhibitor potency.

## Takeaways

- Active-site gorge and peripheral anionic site (PAS) geometry is now structurally characterized for PxAChE1
- PAS entrance is intact in resistant mutants — an allosteric peptide targeting the PAS would not be affected by A298S/G324A
- Homology model quality validated by MD simulation — usable as BoltzGen receptor input with confidence
- Target PAS rather than catalytic triad to design binders insensitive to existing resistance mutations
