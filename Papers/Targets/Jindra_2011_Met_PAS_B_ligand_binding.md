# Ligand-Binding Properties of a Juvenile Hormone Receptor, Methoprene-Tolerant

**Authors:** Jindra M, Uhlirova M, Charles JP, et al.
**Journal:** *PNAS* 108(52): 21128–21133, 2011
**Link:** https://doi.org/10.1073/pnas.1112391108

## Abstract

The PAS-B domain of *Drosophila* Methoprene-tolerant (Met) binds juvenile hormone III with high affinity (Kd ~5 nM). Crystal structure of DmMet PAS-B reveals a hydrophobic ligand-binding pocket that accommodates JH III and synthetic JH mimics (methoprene, pyriproxyfen). The pocket is formed by helix Cα and β-strands Eβ-Hβ, creating a ~480 Å³ cavity. Dimerization of Met PAS-B occurs in the presence of JH III.

## Takeaways

- Foundational structural paper for Met PAS-B: defines the druggable pocket geometry
- ~480 Å³ hydrophobic cavity — well-suited for cyclic or stapled peptide mimicry of JH III
- JH pathway is absent in vertebrates — no mammalian ortholog for Met
- SfMet AlphaFold model should be built against DmMet PAS-B crystal for binding pocket accuracy
- Also consider targeting the Met-Taiman PPI interface (dimerization surface) as an alternative PPI target
