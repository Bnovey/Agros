# CRISPR-Mediated Knockout of Nicotinic Acetylcholine Receptor α6 Subunit Confers High Levels of Resistance to Spinosyns in *Spodoptera frugiperda*

**Authors:** Wang B, Taning CNT, Smagghe G, et al.
**Journal:** *Pesticide Biochemistry and Physiology* 187: 105214, 2022
**Link:** https://doi.org/10.1016/j.pestbp.2022.105214

## Abstract

CRISPR/Cas9 knockout of the Sfα6 subunit of nAChR in *S. frugiperda* confers >300-fold resistance to spinosad and spinetoram, confirming Sfα6 as the exclusive spinosyn target in fall armyworm. Knockout lines showed no fitness cost under laboratory conditions. Electrophysiology confirmed the complete loss of spinosad-evoked currents in α6-null insects.

## Takeaways

- Sfα6 is the exclusive spinosad target in *S. frugiperda* — clean genetic validation
- >300-fold resistance from full knockout = high essentiality for pesticide binding
- No fitness cost in knockouts, but absence of receptor still compatible with survival — means disrupting Sfα6 alone may not kill; consider combination with other targets
- ECD structure: AlphaFold model available; use mammalian nAChR ECD templates (PDB: 2QC1) for binding pocket modeling
