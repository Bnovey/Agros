# Cloning, Ligand-Binding, and Temporal Expression of Ecdysteroid Receptors in the Diamondback Moth, *Plutella xylostella*

**Authors:** Zhao G, Li Z, Li C, et al.
**Journal:** *BMC Molecular Biology* 13: 32, 2012
**Link:** https://doi.org/10.1186/1471-2199-13-32

## Abstract

PxEcR and PxUSP were cloned and expressed in sf9 cells. Heterodimeric PxEcR/PxUSP binds ponasterone A with Kd = 2.9 nM. Expression of both genes peaks in the wandering larval stage preceding pupation. Competitive binding assays with ecdysone analogs (tebufenozide, methoxyfenozide) confirmed functional ligand-binding domain with pharmacological profiles matching the insect molting hormone receptor.

## Takeaways

- Kd = 2.9 nM for ponasterone A — very high affinity pocket, well-validated for tight-binding design
- PxEcR/PxUSP heterodimerization: both the LBD ligand pocket and the EcR-USP dimerization interface are druggable
- Ecdysone receptors are invertebrate-selective (vertebrate RXR/RAR have divergent binding pockets)
- EcR-USP DBD structure available (PDB: 1R0O) for template; AF models for PxEcR and PxUSP
- Design approach: (1) peptide competing with ecdysone in LBD pocket, or (2) stapled peptide disrupting EcR/USP heterodimerization helix
