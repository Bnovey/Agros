# Equivalent Intensity but Differential Dominance of Sodium Channel Blocker Insecticide Resistance Conferred by F1845Y and V1848I Mutations in *Plutella xylostella*

**Authors:** Zhao P, Cao Y, Wang J, et al.
**Journal:** *Insect Biochemistry and Molecular Biology* 145: 103775, 2022
**Link:** https://doi.org/10.1016/j.ibmb.2022.103775

## Abstract

F1845Y and V1848I mutations in the voltage-gated sodium channel (para) of *P. xylostella* confer high-level resistance to indoxacarb and metaflumizone (sodium channel blockers). Both mutations are in Domain IV transmembrane segment 6 (IVS6). AlphaFold2 homology models confirmed the mutations alter the inner pore binding cavity. Resistance is recessively inherited for F1845Y and semidominantly for V1848I.

## Takeaways

- F1845Y and V1848I precisely localize the SCB binding site in the transmembrane inner pore (IVS6 segment)
- AlphaFold2 model used for structure-based analysis — confirms model quality is sufficient for BoltzGen
- Nav channels have vertebrate orthologs, so selectivity must come from insect-specific extracellular loops or divergent residues near the binding site
- De-prioritize vs. PxRyR and OAR targets unless cross-resistance to Nav blockers is specifically desired
