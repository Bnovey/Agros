# Crystal Structure of Ryanodine Receptor N-Terminal Domain from *Plutella xylostella* Reveals Two Potential Species-Specific Insecticide-Targeting Sites

**Authors:** Zhao G, Li Z, Tao Y, et al.
**Journal:** *Insect Biochemistry and Molecular Biology* 92: 73–83, 2018
**Link:** https://doi.org/10.1016/j.ibmb.2017.11.009
**PDB:** 5Y9V (2.84 Å resolution)

## Abstract

Crystal structure of the N-terminal domain (NTD) of *P. xylostella* RyR was solved at 2.84 Å. Structural comparison with mammalian RyR1 and RyR2 identified two pockets in the PxRyR NTD that are present only in insect RyRs — "hot-spot A" and "hot-spot B." These sites are formed by insect-specific loop insertions not found in vertebrate RyRs. Molecular docking with chlorantraniliprole showed preferential binding to hot-spot A.

## Takeaways

- Only Px-specific crystal structure on the 20-target list — highest structural confidence for BoltzGen input
- Two insect-specific pockets (hot-spot A, hot-spot B) are the most insecticide-selective surfaces available — design peptides here for maximum selectivity
- Resistance mutations (I4790K, G4946E) in the transmembrane gating region complement NTD data — design binders against the NTD pockets to avoid resistance
- Critical: PDB 5Y9V is directly usable as BoltzGen receptor input without homology modeling
