# Molecular and Kinetic Characterization of Two Acetylcholinesterases with Particular Focus on the Roles of Two Amino Acid Substitutions (Y390N and F392W) in *Bemisia tabaci*

**Authors:** Toda S, Komazaki S, Tomita T, et al.
**Journal:** *Insect Biochemistry and Molecular Biology* 143: 103737, 2022
**Link:** https://doi.org/10.1016/j.ibmb.2022.103737

## Abstract

*B. tabaci* expresses two AChE isozymes (ace1-type BtAChE1, ace2-type BtAChE2). BtAChE1 carries two unique substitutions — Y390N (previously undescribed in insects) and F392W — in the acyl-binding pocket (the "backdoor" of the active-site gorge). Kinetic analysis shows Y390N significantly reduces catalytic efficiency (kcat/Km drops 3–5-fold) while increasing resistance to OP inhibitors. F392W expands the backdoor opening, suggesting alternate substrate access. Neither mutation is found in other hemipteran AChEs.

## Takeaways

- Y390N is unique to *B. tabaci* — provides a selectivity handle for Bt-specific peptide binder design
- Backdoor (acyl-binding pocket / F392W region) is structurally distinct from Px and vertebrate AChEs — potential insect-selective binding site
- Target BtAChE1 backdoor or PAS with a peptide that exploits the expanded Y390N/F392W cavity
- BtAChE1 AlphaFold model available; Torpedo AChE (PDB: 1ACJ) as homology template
