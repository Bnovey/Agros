# Pharmacological Characterization of a β-Adrenergic-Like Octopamine Receptor in *Plutella xylostella*

**Authors:** Huang QT, Wu C, Tang R, et al.
**Journal:** *Archives of Insect Biochemistry and Physiology* 98(4): e21466, 2018
**Link:** https://doi.org/10.1002/arch.21466

## Abstract

PxOA2B1 (PxOctβ1) was cloned from *P. xylostella* and expressed in HEK-293 cells. Octopamine activates it with EC50 = 49.5 nM, increasing intracellular cAMP. Tyramine is a partial agonist (EC50 ~500 nM). The receptor is expressed throughout all developmental stages and tissues. Amitraz and its metabolites act as agonists. Phenyl imidazolidinone compounds act as antagonists (see Zhu 2021 companion paper).

## Takeaways

- Functional characterization complete: EC50, cAMP signaling pathway, tissue expression all confirmed
- No vertebrate ortholog for octopamine receptors — highest intrinsic selectivity on this target list
- 7TM GPCR; design peptide binders against extracellular loops (EL2/EL3) or the vestibule of the orthosteric site
- Antagonists already validated (Zhu 2021) — confirms druggability and provides a binding-site scaffold for comparison
- Use β2AR (PDB: 2RH1) as template for homology modeling of the TM bundle and ligand-binding pocket
