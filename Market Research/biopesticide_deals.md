# Biopesticide Partnership & Licensing Deals

Reference document for understanding deal structure, timing, and value in the biopesticide space. Sorted by stage at time of deal — earliest to latest.

---

## Stage 1 — Platform / Pre-Data Deals

These deals were signed based on technology platform alone, before significant efficacy data existed.

### Joyn Bio — Bayer + Ginkgo Bioworks JV
- **Year:** 2017 (announced), 2018 (operations launched)
- **Stage:** Pre-product. Platform technology only (synthetic biology for nitrogen fixation).
- **Structure:** Joint venture. Bayer contributed microbial strain archive + field development capabilities. Ginkgo contributed organism design platform + lab space.
- **Value:** $100M Series A (Bayer, Ginkgo, Viking Global Investors)
- **Outcome:** Joyn Bio folded into Ginkgo Bioworks in 2022 when Bayer restructured its biologicals R&D.
- **Notes:** The largest early-stage biologicals platform bet ever made. Required Ginkgo's existing platform credibility — not replicable for a two-person team, but sets the ceiling on what platform deals can look like.

---

### Ginkgo Bioworks — Bayer West Sacramento Acquisition
- **Year:** 2022
- **Stage:** R&D platform acquisition. No new product, Bayer's existing biologics R&D infrastructure.
- **Structure:** Asset acquisition + multi-year R&D services contract.
- **Value:** ~$83M for the facility + team. Multi-year contract (largest in Ginkgo history at the time) + royalties on future product sales.
- **Outcome:** Became Ginkgo's agriculture biologicals business unit, led by Michael Miille.
- **Notes:** Bayer effectively paid Ginkgo to take over its biologicals R&D. Unusual structure driven by Bayer's portfolio rationalization.

## Stage 2 — Lead Identification / AI-Discovered Candidates

Deals signed after computational discovery but before significant wet-lab validation.

### Lavie Bio (Evogene) — Corteva Agriscience Licensing Deal
- **Year:** July 2023
- **Stage:** Lead identification. AI-discovered biofungicide candidates (fruit rots + powdery mildew) identified through Evogene's MicroBoost AI platform. Pre-commercial, pre-EPA.
- **Structure:** Exclusive licensing. Corteva gets rights to develop and commercialize. Lavie Bio receives upfront payments + milestones + royalties.
- **Value:** $5M upfront ($2.5M at signing, $2.5M on milestone). Additional undisclosed milestones + royalties.
- **Outcome:** Agreement terminated by Corteva in November 2024. Lavie Bio (and platform) subsequently acquired by ICL for $18.75M in 2025.
- **Notes:** **Most directly comparable deal to what Agros is trying to do.** AI-discovered candidates, licensed to a Tier 1 ag company before commercial proof. $5M upfront is the benchmark for a computational biology platform licensing deal at lead stage.

## Stage 3 — Pre-Commercial / Proof of Concept

Deals signed after efficacy data existed but before EPA registration or commercial launch.

### AgroSpheres — FMC Corporation Research Collaboration
- **Year:** 2024
- **Stage:** Pre-commercial. AgroSpheres had proprietary AgriCell delivery platform and early bioinsecticide efficacy data. No EPA registration yet.
- **Structure:** Multi-year research collaboration. FMC provides high-throughput testing, evaluation, and go-to-market; AgroSpheres provides delivery platform and RNA portfolio.
- **Value:** Undisclosed. AgroSpheres raised $37M Series B around the same time.
- **Notes:** FMC's interest was in AgroSpheres' delivery technology — the mechanism, not a specific product. Relevant frame for Agros: your platform (generative peptide design) may be licensable before you have a specific commercial product.

### AgroSpheres — BASF Partnership
- **Year:** 2024
- **Stage:** Pre-commercial (same as above).
- **Structure:** Undisclosed. Strategic partnership to expand market reach.
- **Value:** Undisclosed.

### Biotalys — Multiple EU/US Partnerships (pre-EVOCA)
- **Year:** 2021–2024
- **Stage:** Pre-registration (EVOCA™ received EPA approval December 2025).
- **Structure:** Co-development and distribution agreements in Europe and US while registration was pending.
- **Value:** Undisclosed individually. Biotalys raised ~€65M total to fund through registration.
- **Notes:** Biotalys signed distribution agreements before EPA approval — partners were betting on the regulatory outcome. This is a useful model: lock in distribution partners while EPA is reviewing.

## Stage 4 — Post-Registration / Commercial

Deals signed after EPA registration was in hand.

### Vestaron — Wilbur-Ellis Distribution Agreement
- **Year:** May 2020
- **Stage:** Post-EPA registration. SPEAR-LEP® already registered and validated.
- **Structure:** Semi-exclusive distribution agreement. Wilbur-Ellis distributes SPEAR-LEP across North America.
- **Value:** Undisclosed. Vestaron later raised $92M Series C (2022) with Wilbur-Ellis, Novo, and Syngenta participating.
- **Notes:** Vestaron used the Wilbur-Ellis deal as market validation to raise their Series C. Distribution deals can be structured to create proof points for fundraising.

### Syngenta — Bionema Bioinsecticide Acquisition
- **Year:** January 2022
- **Stage:** Post-registration or late-stage registration. NemaTrident® and UniSpore® were fully developed products.
- **Structure:** Outright acquisition of two specific products (not the whole company).
- **Value:** Undisclosed.
- **Notes:** Syngenta acquired specific assets, not the whole company. Bionema (UK) retained its other IP and operations. Shows that large companies will acquire individual product rights even from small companies.

## Stage 5 — Commercial / Acquisition

### Biobest — BioWorks Acquisition
- **Year:** May 2023 (LOI), July 2023 (close)
- **Stage:** Fully commercial. BioWorks had an established portfolio of biopesticide products generating revenue.
- **Structure:** Full acquisition.
- **Value:** Undisclosed.

### ICL — Lavie Bio Acquisition
- **Year:** 2025
- **Stage:** Platform + early products. Lavie Bio had its AI platform and biofungicide candidates (the Corteva deal having terminated).
- **Value:** $18.75M
- **Notes:** Acquired after the Corteva deal fell through. $18.75M for an AI-driven ag biologicals platform with candidates but no commercial product. Sobering — and shows that platform value alone (without commercial traction) has limits.

### Marrone Bio + Bioceres Merger
- **Year:** 2022
- **Stage:** Both companies fully commercial.
- **Structure:** All-stock merger.
- **Value:** ~$50M implied (market cap at time of deal).

## Implications for Agros

**The Lavie Bio → Corteva deal is your benchmark.** AI-discovered biological candidates, licensed to a Tier 1 ag company before commercial proof: $5M upfront. That's what an LOI at our current stage could realistically look like.

**What you need before that deal is reachable:**
- Efficacy data (Pam's point: companies won't sign without it)
- A specific target with validated activity (not just a platform claim)
- EPA-registrability argument (mechanism of action that fits biopesticide criteria)

**What you don't need:**
- EPA registration (the Lavie Bio deal, AgroSpheres deals, and Biotalys pre-registration deals all show companies will sign earlier)
- Commercial revenue
- A full manufacturing process (though you need a credible story)

**Platform deals (Joyn Bio, Ginkgo/Bayer) require institutional credibility** — they don't happen for two-person teams. Product/candidate deals (Lavie Bio, Bionema) are the right model.
