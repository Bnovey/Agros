# Childhood Cancer Rates in Santa Cruz County

**Type:** Market research — public health data
**Date compiled:** 2026-05-19
**Relevance:** Demonstrates harm from incumbent synthetic pesticides; supports Agros value proposition and regulatory tailwinds

---

## Key Statistics

| Metric                                  | Value                                 |
| --------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------- |
| Santa Cruz County pediatric cancer rate | 22.5 per 100,000 children (ages 0–14) |
| California statewide rate               | 16.3 per 100,000 children             |
| Excess above state average              | **+38%**                              |
| State ranking                           | **#2 in California** by county        |
| Data period                             | 2017–2021                             |
| Source                                  | National Cancer Institute             |

Santa Cruz County's rate is not a marginal outlier — it is the second-highest of all 58 California counties by a significant margin.

## The Pesticide Connection

**UCLA Fielding School of Public Health** identified 13 pesticides correlated with childhood cancer onset (birth to age 5) when mothers lived within 2.5 miles of applications during pregnancy.

### Annual acreage of those 13 pesticides in Santa Cruz County (avg. 2017–2021)

| Pesticide                      | Type                        | Acres/year            |
| ------------------------------ | --------------------------- | --------------------- |
| Propiconazole                  | Fungicide (triazole)        | 967                   |
| Chlorothalonil                 | Fungicide                   | 642                   |
| Thiophanate-methyl             | Fungicide                   | 650                   |
| Dimethoate                     | Organophosphate insecticide | 523                   |
| Paraquat dichloride            | Herbicide (highly toxic)    | 199                   |
| Phosmet                        | Organophosphate insecticide | 24                    |
| Others (linuron, diuron, etc.) | Various                     | ~15                   |
| **Total**                      |                             | **~3,020 acres/year** |

A 2024 Nebraska study additionally identified **dicamba and glyphosate** as having the strongest associations with childhood cancers — if included, total reaches ~5,060 acres/year (~8 sq miles) of cancer-linked pesticide application annually in Santa Cruz County alone.

**Source:** California Department of Pesticide Regulation

## Fumigants: The Drift Problem

Fumigants (1,3-dichloropropene/Telone, chloropicrin, metam-sodium) are injected into soil and volatilize as gas, drifting for miles. School-day application restrictions exist but 1,3-D can linger 72+ hours.

**Nine Pajaro Valley schools** have agricultural fields immediately adjacent, including:
- Pajaro Middle School
- Ann Soldo Elementary School
- MacQuiddy Elementary School

Roughly 80% of pesticide weight applied to California strawberries comes from fumigants.

---

## Agricultural Context

- **Driscoll's** dominates Pajaro Valley (the strawberry growing region within Santa Cruz County)
- The broader berry sector generates **$4.5B annually**
- Santa Cruz County produced 87,570 tons of strawberries on 2,640 acres in 2024, valued at $218.9M
- Only **14% of county strawberries are currently organic** — up from near-zero in the 1980s
- Organic yield is ~70% of conventional, making the conversion commercially viable

---

## Implications for Agros

- **Market pull:** There is documented, quantified harm from incumbent synthetic pesticides in the exact geography where our target crops (strawberries, leafy greens) are grown. This is not hypothetical risk.
- **Regulatory tailwind:** Public pressure on Santa Cruz County and the Pajaro Valley specifically is intensifying. EPA and state-level restrictions on fumigants (1,3-D, chloropicrin) are expanding. Biopesticides bypass this risk.
- **Narrative:** The 38% excess cancer rate in a single county is a credible, citable statistic for YC/investor decks and press without overstating causation — the UCLA correlation study is peer-reviewed.
- **Caveat:** Correlation, not proven causation. The mechanism (prenatal exposure within 2.5 miles) is plausible and consistent with other agricultural county studies, but causation is contested by industry. Frame as "correlated risk" not "proven cause."
