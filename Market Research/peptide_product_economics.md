# Peptide Biopesticide Product Economics

Estimated revenue and profit for a single peptide biopesticide that reaches commercial market. Based on comparable products and deal structures in the biopesticide space.

---

## Revenue — What the Product Actually Generates

A biopesticide product lives in a crop/pest niche. For a lepidopteran-targeting peptide (nAChR target, Agros's primary):

| Scenario | Treated Acres (US) | Price/Acre | Annual Revenue |
|---|---|---|---|
| Conservative | 500K | $20 | $10M |
| Base | 2M | $25 | $50M |
| Optimistic | 5M | $30 | $150M |

Most successful niche biopesticides land in the **$10–50M/year range at maturity** (5–10 years post-launch). A blockbuster (Bt-level) hits $100M+, but that's rare.

**Comp:** Vestaron's SPEAR products are targeting this range. They raised $92M Series C (2022) against this revenue potential.

---

## Gross Margin — Depends on Manufacturing

| Method | Cost/kg at Scale | Gross Margin |
|---|---|---|
| Chemical peptide synthesis | $500–5K/kg | 15–30% |
| Recombinant fermentation (yeast/bacteria) | $50–500/kg | 35–55% |
| Licensed to partner (royalties only) | ~zero COGS | ~100% of royalty rate |

Fermentation is the target manufacturing model. Vestaron used this approach via their ADM manufacturing partnership — it's how you get margins above 40%. Chemical synthesis is too expensive at scale for a low-margin agricultural product.

---

## What Agros Actually Sees (Licensing Model)

Agros is not selling to farmers — it's licensing to a Tier 1 ag company (Corteva, Syngenta, FMC). In that structure:

| Payment Type | Amount | Timing |
|---|---|---|
| Upfront at signing | $2.5–5M | At LOI / license execution |
| Milestone — EPA registration | $1–5M | ~3–5 years post-signing |
| Milestone — first commercial sale | $1–3M | ~5–8 years post-signing |
| Royalties | 5–12% of net sales | Ongoing at maturity |

**On a $20M/year product:** $1–2.4M/year in royalties at maturity.
**Total deal value (NPV):** $10–30M for a modestly successful product; $50M+ for a broad market product.

**Benchmark:** Lavie Bio → Corteva: $5M upfront for AI-discovered biofungicide candidates, pre-EPA. Terminated before royalties kicked in.

---

## Timeline to Revenue

| Stage | Typical Duration |
|---|---|
| Discovery → wet lab validation | 1–2 years |
| Wet lab → EPA registration (biopesticide) | 2–4 years |
| EPA registration → commercial launch | 1–2 years |
| Commercial launch → meaningful revenue | 2–4 years |
| **Total: discovery to $10M+ revenue** | **6–12 years** |

EPA biopesticide registration is faster and cheaper than conventional pesticides (~$1–3M vs. $250M+), but still takes 2–4 years.

---

## The Honest Picture

- **~90% of candidates never reach commercial launch.** The licensing model transfers this risk to the partner — they fund development, Agros collects milestones + royalties if it works.
- **The real value capture is in the platform**, not a single product. A platform that generates multiple candidates is worth more than any one deal.
- **Agros's near-term target is the LOI/licensing deal** — $2.5–5M upfront — not the downstream royalty stream. That's the proof point that funds wet lab buildout and positions for Series A.
