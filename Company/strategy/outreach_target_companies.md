# Outreach Target Companies

Companies to contact for partnerships, validation, and LOIs. Sorted by fit with Agros.

---

## Tier 1 — Highest fit (peptide / AI / computational focus)

### Vestaron
- **What they do:** Peptide-based bioinsecticides derived from spider venom, manufactured via fermentation. Two active ingredients now EPA-approved (SPEAR® portfolio + BASIN™).
- **Why relevant:** Direct overlap with our peptide approach. They've proven the regulatory and commercial path. Could be a licensing or co-development conversation.
- **Status:** Well-funded ($287M total raised, $20M+ round closed March 2025). Commercially scaling in US and Europe.
- **Target contact:** Head of R&D or External Innovation
- **Website:** vestaron.com

### Invaio Sciences (acquired Peptyde Bio)
- **What they do:** Nature-positive crop protection platform. Acquired Peptyde Bio (AI-discovered AMPs against *Botrytis cinerea*) in December 2023. Also working on novel delivery systems for biologicals.
- **Why relevant:** They own the closest comparable technology to ours — AI-designed antifungal peptides. CSO Magalie Guilhabert joined May 2025 from Ginkgo/Bayer, understands the computational angle.
- **Status:** Well-funded, HQ in Cambridge MA. CEO Amy O'Shea since 2024.
- **Target contact:** Magalie Guilhabert (CSO) or Amy O'Shea (CEO)
- **Website:** invaio.com

### Evogene
- **What they do:** Computational biology platform company. Uses AI to discover and optimize biological active ingredients. Has subsidiaries across insecticides, herbicides, and microbiome.
- **Why relevant:** They understand computational discovery pipelines and have partnered with large ag companies (Bayer, Corteva, Syngenta, Marrone Bio). Would immediately understand what we're doing.
- **Status:** Publicly traded (NASDAQ: EVGN). Sold Lavie Bio to ICL in 2025 — may be open to new platform partnerships.
- **Target contact:** VP of Business Development or head of the relevant subsidiary
- **Website:** evogene.com

## Tier 2 — Active partnership history, open to external tech

### Certis Biologicals
- **What they do:** One of the largest biopesticide distributors in North America. Portfolio includes biofungicides, bioinsecticides, bionematicides. Known for partnering with small biotech companies to bring products to market.
- **Why relevant:** Have commercialized products from external partners repeatedly (Novozymes, W. Neudorff, OmniLytics, Andermatt). A natural route-to-market partner once we have validated leads.
- **Status:** ~137 employees, Mitsui-owned. Recorded record sales in 2025.
- **Target contact:** Director of External Innovation or Business Development
- **Website:** certisbio.com

### Pro Farm Group (formerly Marrone Bio Innovations)
- **What they do:** Large biologicals portfolio — bioinsecticides, biofungicides, bionematicides, biostimulants. 18,000+ microorganism library, 500+ patents. Merged with Bioceres in 2022.
- **Why relevant:** Has partnered externally before (Evogene, Koch, Syngenta, FMC). Could be interested in our peptide design platform as a pipeline addition.
- **Status:** Global, headquartered in Raleigh NC. Introduced new bacterial agent (RinoTec™) in March 2025.
- **Target contact:** Head of R&D or External Partnerships
- **Website:** marronebio.com

### Andermatt Biocontrol
- **What they do:** Swiss company, one of the largest baculovirus manufacturers globally. Broad biocontrol portfolio across insects, fungi, rodents. Active in 150+ countries.
- **Why relevant:** Strong in Europe, active BPIA member, has partnered with Certis for US commercialization. Interested in new MoAs.
- **Status:** Privately held, been operating since 1988. Has a US presence (Andermatt USA).
- **Target contact:** Head of R&D or US business lead
- **Website:** andermattbiocontrol.com

## Tier 3 — Worth a shot

### Lavie Bio (now part of ICL)
- **What they do:** AI-driven microbial biostimulant and biopesticide discovery. Was Evogene subsidiary, acquired by ICL (Israeli chemicals company) in Q2 2025.
- **Why relevant:** They understand AI-driven ag biology. Post-acquisition, ICL may be looking to expand the platform — could be an interesting conversation.
- **Status:** Transition period post-acquisition. Approach ICL's ag biologicals team.
- **Target contact:** ICL Crop Nutrition / ag biologicals division
- **Website:** lavie-bio.com

### Valent BioSciences
- **What they do:** Biopesticide and plant growth regulator manufacturer. Strong in insecticides (Bacillus-based) and fungicides.
- **Why relevant:** Marquee player in biologicals, listed alongside Corteva in market analyses. Less known for external partnerships but worth contacting.
- **Target contact:** R&D or External Innovation
- **Website:** valentbiosciences.com

### AgroSpheres
- **What they do:** Controlled-release biopesticide delivery using proprietary AgriCell encapsulation technology. UVA spinout.
- **Why relevant:** Complementary tech — if our peptides need a delivery system, this is a natural conversation. Early stage, open to collaboration.
- **Status:** Early stage startup
- **Target contact:** Founders directly
