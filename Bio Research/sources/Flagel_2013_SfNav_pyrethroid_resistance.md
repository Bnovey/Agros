# Investigating the Molecular Mechanisms of Organophosphate and Pyrethroid Resistance in the Fall Armyworm *Spodoptera frugiperda*

**Authors:** Flagel L, Lee Y, Bingham M, et al.
**Journal:** *PLOS ONE* 8(4): e62268, 2013
**Link:** https://doi.org/10.1371/journal.pone.0062268

## Abstract

Transcriptome and population-genetics analysis of field-collected *S. frugiperda* identified kdr mutations (T929I, L932F, L1014F) in the para-type voltage-gated sodium channel as the primary basis of pyrethroid resistance. L1014F (homologous to the classic kdr allele in many insects) was at highest frequency. Metabolic resistance via CYP450 upregulation was also detected but accounted for less of the variance.

## Takeaways

- kdr mutations T929I, L932F, L1014F localize the pyrethroid binding site in Domain II transmembrane segments (IIS4-IIS5 linker and IIS6)
- These residues define the surface geometry for competing peptide binder design
- Nav channels are lower priority vs. insect-selective targets (vertebrate orthologs present)
- Use as a selectivity guide: focus peptide design on insect-specific extracellular loops rather than the pyrethroid transmembrane site to avoid mammalian off-target binding
