# Nanomaterial-Mediated RNAi Targeting Chitin Metabolism Genes in MEAM1 Cryptic Species of *Bemisia tabaci* (Hemiptera: Aleyrodidae)

**Authors:** Guo W, Zhang C, Li X, et al.
**Journal:** *Insects* 17(1): 2, 2026
**Link:** https://doi.org/10.3390/insects17010002

## Abstract

dsRNA targeting BtCHS2 delivered via clay nanosheets achieved ~80% nymph mortality at 48h in *B. tabaci* MEAM1. BtCHS2 encodes a chitin synthase specific to the peritrophic matrix of the midgut. Expression is highest in nymphs and adults; absent in eggs. Knockdown phenotype showed midgut integrity disruption. No toxicity to honeybees or *Arabidopsis* plants.

## Takeaways

- ~80% mortality at 48h from RNAi — confirms BtCHS2 is essential and gut-accessible for knockdown-based approaches
- Midgut-specific expression = natural delivery route for oral peptides targeting the gut epithelium
- No honeybee toxicity confirmed — important safety validation for a biopesticide
- BtCHS2 AlphaFold model available; no crystal structure
- CHS2 vs CHS1: CHS2 is the gut/peritrophic matrix isoform; CHS1 is the cuticle isoform. BtCHS2 is better for oral delivery.
