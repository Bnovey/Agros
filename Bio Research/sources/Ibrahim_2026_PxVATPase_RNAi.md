# RNAi-Mediated Silencing of vATPase Subunit E Impairs Larval Development in *Plutella xylostella*, and Virtual Screening Identifies a Potential Inhibitor

**Authors:** Ibrahim SS, Ahmed S, Zhang Y, et al.
**Journal:** *Insects* 17(4): 439, 2026
**Link:** https://doi.org/10.3390/insects17040439

## Abstract

RNAi knockdown of vATPase subunit E (PxvATPaseE) in *P. xylostella* larvae caused dose-dependent growth arrest and mortality. Virtual screening against the V1 domain V-ATPase structure identified a compound with IC50 = 18 µM in ATPase activity assays. The V1 domain subunit-E interface was identified as the most druggable protein-protein interface distinct from mammalian V-ATPase assembly.

## Takeaways

- RNAi validation confirms PxvATPaseE is essential for larval development — target is real
- V1 domain PPI interface (subunit E contacts) identified as druggable and divergent from mammalian V-ATPase
- Virtual screening already done — provides a starting geometry for the binding pocket
- Mammalian V-ATPase cryo-EM template: PDB 7SIF; AlphaFold model for PxvATPaseE
- Design peptides targeting the insect-specific inter-subunit contacts in V1, not the catalytic ATPase active site
