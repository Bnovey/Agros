## 2-Minute Pitch

**Problem:**
Botrytis cinerea is the most economically damaging fungal pathogen in agriculture — $10B+ in annual crop losses. Growers have historically managed it with synthetic fungicides, but resistance is now widespread across all major FRAC classes. At the same time, the EU is mandating a 50% cut in synthetic pesticide use by 2030. Growers are running out of options exactly when regulation is pushing them off the tools they have.

**Solution:**
Agros is building a closed-loop AI discovery engine for peptide biopesticides. Our pipeline: BoltzGen generates candidate peptide sequences against specific agricultural targets (starting with Botrytis β-glucan synthase and Lepidopteran nAChR/RyR). Boltz-2 scores binding affinity. Integrated Gradients analysis identifies the 3–5 residues that drive activity — we don't just predict, we understand. Top candidates are synthesized at $2–4/amino acid and validated in vitro. Those results feed back into the next model generation.

**Why now:**
Boltz-2 and BoltzGen are new as of 2024–2025. Peptide synthesis costs have dropped 80% in a decade. EU Farm to Fork regulation is creating urgency. These three things converged and created a window.

## Framing Notes

- Lead with Botrytis resistance data — it's visceral and the numbers are shocking (94% resistance to thiophanate-methyl)
- "We understand *why* it works" is the key differentiator — say it simply, don't say "integrated gradients"
- $10B+ in losses + all fungicide classes failing = the problem sells itself; don't rush to the solution
- Anchor to real biology: Botrytis β-glucan synthase is the target, not a vague "fungal pathogen"
