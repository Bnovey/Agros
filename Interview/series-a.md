# Series A — What Unlocks the Next Round

---

## The Milestone That Triggers a Series A

**Single clear trigger:** In vivo field efficacy data for ≥1 peptide active against Botrytis cinerea on a commercial specialty crop (grapes, strawberries, or ornamentals).

Why this specifically:
- In vitro → in vivo is the biggest de-risking step in biopesticide development
- Field data gives agchem licensing partners a reason to engage at serious deal terms
- It's a publishable result (peer-reviewed or bioRxiv preprint) — scientific credibility that compounds
- EPA biopesticide registration requires in vivo efficacy data — this starts the regulatory clock
- Series A investors in agtech need to see proof the biology works outside a lab

**Secondary signals that accompany it:**
- ≥1 LOI or licensing term sheet from an agchem biologicals division or specialty biopesticide distributor
- BioRxiv preprint live (the attention/IG analysis paper — scientific moat signal)
- Pipeline expanded to ≥2 targets with in vitro data

## What Makes This a Good Series A Story (vs. What Kills It)

**Good:**
- In vivo data exists and is real — not projected
- At least one licensing conversation active (even if not closed)
- The scientific paper is live — external validation of the platform
- Platform has shown it can work on ≥2 different targets (not a one-hit wonder)

**Bad (avoid these situations):**
- Series A with only in vitro data — rare exceptions exist but it's a harder raise
- No commercial traction signals — pure science without market validation
- Pipeline of one — if the lead candidate doesn't work, investors worry the whole platform is unproven
- Competitor has registered a similar peptide class — creates IP uncertainty
