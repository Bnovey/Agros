## TAM / SAM / SOM

**TAM — Total Addressable Market**
> The global biopesticide market is $7–8B today, growing at 12–15% CAGR to $14–18B by 2030.
> Broader frame: the $60B+ crop protection market is undergoing a secular shift to biologicals driven by regulation and resistance.

**SAM — Serviceable Addressable Market**
> Antifungal biopesticides for specialty crops (fruits, vegetables, ornamentals) in the US and EU.
> - Botrytis alone causes >$10B in annual crop losses globally
> - US + EU specialty crop biofungicide spend: estimated **$400–800M today**, growing rapidly
> - Biofungicide segment growing at 11% CAGR vs. 5% for conventional fungicides

**SOM — Serviceable Obtainable Market**
> In 3–5 years: 1–2% of SAM via licensing deals with distributors or agchem partners = **$5–15M ARR**
> Path: 1–2 validated actives licensed at $1–3M upfront + royalties, with field trial data from specialty crop pilots

## Why Now

- **Regulatory tailwinds:** EU Farm to Fork targets 50% reduction in synthetic pesticide use and 50% reduction in hazardous pesticides by 2030. 27% reduction in hazardous pesticides already achieved by 2023 — growers are scrambling for alternatives. Key SUR legislation in flux but direction is irreversible.
- **Resistance crisis — it's worse than people realize:** B. cinerea has developed resistance to **all currently registered single-site fungicides**. Michigan greenhouse studies (2024): 94% of isolates resistant to thiophanate-methyl, 80% to pyraclostrobin, 67% to boscalid. 63.5% of isolates resistant to ≥4 FRAC groups. Growers are losing their tools.
- **AI + protein ML maturity:** AlphaFold, ESM-2, Boltz-2 — the infrastructure to do interpretable peptide design at scale now exists. 5 years ago it didn't.
- **Peptide synthesis cost collapse:** Custom peptide synthesis now starts at $2–4/amino acid (ProteoGenix, AAPPTEC, others). A 20-residue candidate costs $50–100 to synthesize — 80%+ cheaper than a decade ago.
- **Big agchem is buying, not building:** BASF acquired Becker Underwood, Bayer acquired AgraQuest — they validate the market but can't pivot their 10-year R&D pipelines.

## Market Dynamics

- Conventional fungicide manufacturers know resistance is spreading — they're under-investing in new actives and over-invested in legacy chemistry
- Specialty crop growers pay a premium for effective alternatives — higher gross margins and more immediate switching behavior than commodity row crop markets
- Biofungicide segment is structurally underserved: $3.2B market with no clear peptide-first player
- **Regulatory approval pathway for peptide biopesticides:**
  - Novel peptides: EPA full biopesticide registration (biochemical pesticide category) — typically 2–4 years, lower data requirements than conventional pesticides
  - If peptide qualifies as minimum-risk: EPA 25(b) exemption (no federal registration required, state-by-state)
  - Organic certification: OMRI listing (separate from EPA, required for organic market access)
  - Recommendation: pursue 25(b) exemption path first if peptide ingredients qualify, then full registration for scale
