# Business Model

## Revenue Model

### Our path: Option B → Option A (licensing now, product later)

**Phase 1 — Licensing (YC batch + 18 months):**
- Discover and validate novel peptide actives against Botrytis β-glucan synthase and Lepidopteran targets
- License IP to agchem biologicals divisions (Bayer, BASF, Syngenta, Corteva) or specialty biopesticide companies
- Deal structure: upfront payment + development milestones + royalties on net sales
- Revenue: faster than building a product — can sign LOIs pre-product with in vivo field data

**Phase 2 — Product (24–36 months, post-series A):**
- Formulate proprietary peptide actives into crop protection products
- Sell to specialty crop growers or through distributors
- Higher margin if IP-protected; regulatory approval needed

**Why licensing first:**
- YC batch is 3 months — not enough time to build a product. But enough time to produce in vitro validation data that gets us into licensing conversations.
- Agchem companies need new actives desperately — they will pay for novel, validated mechanisms before full regulatory approval.
- Licensing funds the wet lab while we build the proprietary dataset moat.

## Pricing

### Licensing deal structure (agchem precedent)
- **Upfront:** $500K–$3M for an early-stage active with in vitro + in vivo data
- **Development milestones:** $2–10M across regulatory and commercial milestones
- **Royalties:** 3–8% of net sales (agchem royalties are lower than pharma)
- **Total deal value for a single active:** $10–50M over full commercialization

### Product pricing (if we go direct)
- Conventional fungicide spray programs: ~$50–150/acre/season for specialty crops
- Premium biopesticide pricing: $100–250/acre/season feasible for highly effective novel mechanisms
- Growers will pay a premium for resistance-breaking actives with no existing alternative

## Go-to-Market

### Phase 1 (now — 12 months): Validate and license
- Produce in vitro efficacy data against Botrytis β-glucan synthase
- Identify top 3–5 peptide candidates with IG-validated mechanism
- 2 grower conversations → pilot spray trials (grapes, strawberries, ornamentals)
- 1 LOI from agchem biologicals division or specialty biopesticide company

### Phase 2 (12–24 months): In vivo field data
- Partner with university extension or contract research org for field trials
- Regulatory pre-submission meeting with EPA (biopesticide division)
- Expand to second target (Lepidopteran nAChR or RyR)
- Series A fundraise on field trial results + licensing conversations

### Phase 3 (24–36 months): Commercial deal or product launch
- First licensing deal signed OR EPA biopesticide registration filed
- Expand pipeline to 3+ targets
- Revenue target: $2–5M ARR (licensing) or first paid product sales

## Unit Economics

### Discovery cost per candidate (current estimates)
- Custom peptide synthesis (20-residue peptide): ~$100–300 (at $2–4/AA + purification, via ProteoGenix / AAPPTEC)
- In vitro antifungal assay (MIC / EC50 vs. Botrytis): ~$500–1,500 (contract lab) or ~$200–500 (university lab partnership)
- IG analysis + scoring (GPU compute via Modal): ~$5–20/candidate on A100
- **Total cost per candidate, synthesized + assayed:** ~$700–2,000
- Expected hit rate for de novo antimicrobial peptides: 10–30% (literature; our interpretability-guided approach should be at the high end)
- **Cost per validated hit:** ~$3,000–15,000 (1–3 validated hits per 10-candidate round)

### Scale economics
- 10-candidate discovery round: ~$7,000–20,000 total
- 50-candidate campaign (5 rounds): ~$35,000–100,000 → expected 5–15 validated hits
- A $500K YC check funds 5–15 full discovery rounds

That milestone gives us:
- Scientific credibility (peer-reviewed or preprint)
- Regulatory foundation (data package for EPA biopesticide submission)
- Licensing leverage (agchem companies will pay for in vivo-validated actives)
- Competitive moat (our proprietary dataset starts compounding)

**Timeline to that milestone:** 12–18 months post-YC, $500K–1.5M in capital
